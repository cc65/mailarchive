At one time I knew the origin of that word, but it escapes me now. It
may have been BLock bIT TransfER. Anyway, I've seen BLIT used as a
verb, and BLITTER to mean the thing that does it, so this explanation
may not hold water.

A BLITTER moves blocks of memory from one place to another, but the
blocks do not have to be on byte boundaries. The first time I heard it
was in reference to a piece of hardware on the Atari 1000 series
computers. They are very useful for graphics.
