European and U.S. ISPs usually get the reverse DNS right, while ISPs in asia
and africa often don't care about unimportant things like this. As a
coincidence, a high percentage of SPAM mails today is relayed over mail
servers outside of the U.S. and europe.

But your ISP seems to have more problems than just this one. My mail server is
often delaying mails to you for days since your MX is not reachable ("no route
to host").

BTW: Most people do not have problems with a missing reverse DNS but with an
invalid ftp password. So it's often not a technical but a user problem.
