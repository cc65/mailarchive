Hello Mark,

> Forgive my non-familiarity of the C64 disk drive but
> how would the server running from its ROM know about
> the filesystem layout? Isn't the drive only working at
> a sector level? The Atari's require a disk operating
> system (DOS) to be booted into RAM to handle such things
> as free/used sectors, directories etc. How the C64/1541
> handle this?

That's exactly the difference between the CBM floppies and other floppies
from the 8 bit era. The CBMs are full featured computers (6502, 6522 or
6526, ...) which handle all the disc stuff *including* the filesystem. The
just receive a command "I want this or that file" and they answer with
exactly this one. They would have been *very* great things if, yes, if they
had not had that *** seriel line interface (not all but many of them). At
least, since these were full-featured computers, one was able to write a
good floppy-speeder to overcome this limit.

Spiro.
