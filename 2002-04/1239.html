For those of you who doesn't read comp.sys.cbm or the cbm-hackers
mailing list:

In order to show off our new TFE Ethernet Cartridge for the C64, we
have connected a TFE-equipped but otherwise unexpanded C64 to the
Internet. It runs the uIP TCP/IP stack (http://dunkels.com/adam/uip/)
that also is used on Uz' C64 at http://c64.cc65.org. It also runs a
real-time audio streaming server which streams audio directly from the
Datasette cassette player.

The software is mostly written in C, with some of the most
CPU-intensive parts written in assembler, and has been compiled with
the cc65 snapshot of April 3.

Our C64 server can be reached at http://tfe.c64.org/ and more
information, including the full source code, can be found at
http://dunkels.com/adam/tfe/.
