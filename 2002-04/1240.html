Friday, April 12, 2002, 3:33:49 PM, you wrote:

AD> For those of you who doesn't read comp.sys.cbm or the cbm-hackers
AD> mailing list:

AD> In order to show off our new TFE Ethernet Cartridge for the C64, we
AD> have connected a TFE-equipped but otherwise unexpanded C64 to the
AD> Internet. It runs the uIP TCP/IP stack (http://dunkels.com/adam/uip/)
AD> that also is used on Uz' C64 at http://c64.cc65.org. It also runs a
AD> real-time audio streaming server which streams audio directly from the
AD> Datasette cassette player.

AD> The software is mostly written in C, with some of the most
AD> CPU-intensive parts written in assembler, and has been compiled with
AD> the cc65 snapshot of April 3.

AD> Our C64 server can be reached at http://tfe.c64.org/ and more
AD> information, including the full source code, can be found at
AD> http://dunkels.com/adam/tfe/.
