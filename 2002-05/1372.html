On Wed, May 22, 2002 at 10:27:56AM +0200, Piotr Fusik wrote:
> IMHO best would be to have IEEE 4-byte floats. Since doubles are very
> common (probably more than floats) I would suggest built-in typedef float
> double. What does the C standard say about it? On many platforms int ==
> long, is it ok if float == double ?

float == double is ok. The standard says (similar as with integers) that the
precision of double must be larger or equal than that of floats. There exist
some (standard compliant) C compilers, that have just one floating point type.
