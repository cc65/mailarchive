On Wednesday 11 December 2002 01:07, Ullrich von Bassewitz wrote:
> On Tue, Dec 10, 2002 at 05:13:09PM +0100, Groepaz wrote:
> > yes ofcourse.... i would take the "some keys" as "normal" behaviour and
> > the "all keys" as the "keyrepeat" behaviour.... the "no repeats" thing
> > isnt much useful anyway :)
>
> I would prefer to have "no repeat" to be "no repeat" and not "some keys
> repeat and others not".

but then its kinda hard to restore the original state...mmmmhh, so then that 
MUST be done in module destructor.

i still like the "some keys" thing better though, since ppl would get the 
standard behaviour they are used to in any editor-type of routine. (and imho 
thats a much more regular use than one were a "no repeat" behavior would be 
better). i know a "no repeat" would be cleaner, but it can be simulated if 
needed... the other way around (simulating repeat for some edit-keys) would 
just regulary add additional work and overhead, much more than a missing 
norepeat mode atleast.
