On Sat, Dec 21, 2002 at 02:16:50AM -0500, Greg King wrote:
> That warning might confuse people.  Instead of "... does not exist," it should
> say that the segments are not used.

Which would be wrong. The warning message is indeed correct and the problem is
that the segments are assigned but there are no such segments.

> And, it should be shown only when ld65's
> verbose-mode is turned on.

It's a warning, and it's important. If someone creates its own linker config,
this warning is important, because it means that there's a high probility that
something is wrong with the config. The reason why it doesn't seem as
important in the given case is that all this happens with a predefined config,
so the user is not aware of it. The correct way to remove the problem is to
fix the config, not to suppress the warning.
