> Yes, that would be nice. There's somewhat more in the dio API,
> but it's a start.  Your implementation is quite portable,
> and should work for other CBMs, too.
>
> One problem, however:  The file-handles choosen may collide
> with the ones used by the CBM file-I/O.  I will have to add a
>
>        #define CBM_FIRST_FREE_HANDLE   xxx
>
> define somewhere in the headers, so programs using the CBM routines
> can safely use CBM file-I/O with handles starting with that number.

The CBM dio functions could be integrated with the POSIX family of
functions.  They could use close() and [the internal code of] open() to
make the "#" buffer file, read() and write() to move the sector-data, and
writediskcmd and readdiskerror to send CBM-DOS's "u1" and "u2" commands.
Then, there would be no conflicts.
