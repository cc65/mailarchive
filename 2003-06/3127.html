On Wed, Jun 18, 2003 at 11:13:14AM +0200, Christian Groessler wrote:
> How about making long 24bit and inventing a long long if a 32 bit type is
> needed?
>
> (I know, it's quick to ask, but probably quite a hassle to implement...:-)

Not only this, but the standard requires the "long" data type to have at least
32 bits (in fact, it doesn't require 32 bit, but a certain range, and this
range means that at least 32 bits are needed). Any compiler where longs are
smaller wouldn't be standards compliant.

A better idea would probably be to introduce "far" pointers with 24 bits. This
would also help as a first step for 65816 code.
