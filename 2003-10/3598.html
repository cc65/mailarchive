Spiro and I have actually been in contact quite regularly during his
excellent work with the TFE emulation, and we've just been discussing
porting the TFE emulation to *nix. The problem with the tap device is
that it doesn't exactly provide the same functionality as the pcap
libraries. The tap device basically creates a virtual LAN, while the
pcap gives direct access to the physical LAN (the one on which the PC is
connected to).

Under FreeBSD it might be possible to use the BPF (Berkeley Packet
Filter) to get the same kind of access as the pcap library under
Windows, but I don't know if this is available under Linux.
