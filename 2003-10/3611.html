Hi!

Because of recent events, I have to bring up a few policy issues. 

As you do probably know, email is not a very reliable medium, which means that
I do have to handle bounces and other mail problems somehow. Unfortunately, if
mail to a list subscriber bounces, this means that I cannot reach him either,
so communication fails exactly in the same moment when it would be needed. So
here is my problem handling policy for the list:

  1. If I do get a bounce with "user unknown", "domain unknown" or similar,
     I will remove the offending address immediately.

  2. On temporary technical problems ("host unreachable", DNS problems), I
     will remove the address after 3-5 days.

  3. User related problems (mailbox full, permanent auto responder messages or
     similar) will cause me to remove the user after 24-48 hours. In some
     situations (e.g. after the Sven virus made many mail accounts unusable
     for days) I will wait somewhat longer.

  4. If the mail server responsible for your address refuses to accept mail
     from the list server, I will try to resolve this issue with the admin of
     the mail server depending on the problem. However, if I don't get a reply
     from the mail server admin within 12-24 hours, I will remove the
     problematic address from the list. If I don't get a reply to my mail at
     all, or if my mail is not accepted for the same reasons, I may disallow
     all subscriptions from this domain.

In most cases you may just resubscribe if you notice that you have been
dropped from the list. In some other cases (4.) your only choices are to use
another mail address when resubscribing, or bug your admin.
