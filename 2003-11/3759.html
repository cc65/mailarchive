On Friday 14 November 2003 10:31 am, Wurttemberg, Raj wrote:
> cprintf ("Hello \0x97 orld! ");
>
> would print an inverse "W" for the \0x97 but it does not. What am I doing
> wrong?

You want "Hello \x97orld!",.  The '\0' is a the escape sequence for a NUL 
character, effective ending the string.  \x is the escape sequence for 
inserting characters in by their hex code.

Now, I don't know if that'll actually print the character for you, I just saw 
a C mistake...
