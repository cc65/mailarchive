>>>>> "WR" == Wurttemberg, Raj <rajw_at_concert.com> writes:

WR> How do I print PETSCII codes? 

I guess the official answer is that you don't with conio, as it's a
crossplatform library. But in practice most of the PETSCII chars are
mapped at 128..255, you can check out the translation code in
cc65/libsrc/c64/cputc.s.

The stdio functions print PETSCII, but they're quite a bit slower than
conio. And you can of course access screen ram directly with something
like unsigned char *screen = 0x0400;
