On Saturday 18 September 2004 16:33, Raj Wurttemberg wrote:

> A little weirdness here. I told cc65 to use $73 but the .byte line in the
> assembly shows it was converted to a $53:
> 
> L0001:
> 	.byte
> $0D,$48,$45,$4C,$4C,$4F,$20,$57,$4F,$52,$4C,$44,$21,$20,$53,$00
> 
> Why did that happen? Am I doing something wrong? If I edit the assembly and
> change the $53 back to a $73 it works properly and displays the heart
> character. 

that happens because cc65 converts the charset encoding (from ascii to petscii
in this case). however, i am not sure if this is correct behaviour here
(when using \x).... no idea what the standard says here.

as a workaround you can do printf ("\nhello world! %c",0x73); ... but thats
kinda ugly, i know :=P UZ might be able to say more about this...
