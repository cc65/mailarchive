There are two reasons for the makefile not being in the top level directory:
The first one is that there is more than one makefile. Cluttering the top
level directory with lots of different makefiles would not help anyone. The
second reason is that I'm trying in general to keep the top level directory
clean. I have refused to add special Debian subdirectories, or the RedHat spec
file to the top level dir (so that the package can be built with -t). I'm
really wondering why everyone thinks that exactly his file/directory is so
important that is has to be on top of anything else.
