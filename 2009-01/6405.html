> To the OP:  in a makefile, commands are supposed to be preceded by
> a tab, not three (or even eight) spaces.  Whatever flavour of make
> Karri uses may accept spaces; but, you can't count on that, as that
> is not standard make syntax.

It is a bad idea to use tabs in E-Mail.  You cannot know how they will be
displayed in someone else's mail-reader.  You cannot know which font will be
used (what is its size, is it mono-spaced or proportional, etc.), and you
cannot know how that reader will show tabs.  So, some E-Mail programs will
convert tabs into a group of spaces when you are creating your mail.
Karri's makefiles do have tabs where they are supposed to be -- they just
don't show them in this mailing-list.
