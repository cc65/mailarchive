> Thanks :-) Two remarks
>
> 1. The non-fluent sed readers might be interested to know that the
> code above reads up to the ':' (known to just preceed the tab) and

>
> 2. While $(<:c=s) (and the other two substitution references)

> $(<:.c=.s) as it nicely resembles the file name extensions in the rule

are we dealing with.
