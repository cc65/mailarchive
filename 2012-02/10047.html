Btw, this is not exactly true. 65CE02 - AFAIK - was a real product, even
used in some kind of Amiga hardware. C65 uses 4510 (or so) which is actually

that 4510's "CPU core" is 65CE02, only one extra opcode was added MAP (for
MMU functionality), though I am not sure what it did on a 65CE02. I don't
know is 4510 was ever released as a chip other than in C65 prototypes.
